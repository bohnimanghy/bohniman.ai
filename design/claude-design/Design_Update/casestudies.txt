Case Study: e-Court Management & Biometric Verification Platform
Client Goal
The client required a large-scale digital governance platform to modernize sensitive legal and administrative workflows across distributed operational departments. Existing processes relied heavily on fragmented manual records, physical files, and disconnected workflows, creating operational delays, limited traceability, and difficulty in tracking repeat suspects who could change identities and relocate across jurisdictions.
The two primary objectives of the project were:
Establish a biometric-enabled digital case initiation system to uniquely identify new suspects and prevent identity manipulation.
Digitize and migrate approximately 0.45 million historical legacy cases accumulated over nearly 27 years into a centralized searchable digital platform.
The solution also needed to support secure workflow orchestration, hearing management, inter-department coordination, centralized monitoring, and long-term archival continuity across geographically distributed operational units.
What We Did
Bohniman Systems Pvt. Ltd. acted as the prime contractor and full system integrator for the project, including software development, data center setup, digitization, deployment, VPN integration, biometric systems integration, training, and onsite operational support.
The project covered:
Digitization of over 5.5 million historical records
Migration of approximately 0.45 million legacy cases
Deployment across 100 tribunal offices
Integration with 319 police stations and 34 district police headquarters
Centralized management of nearly 1 TB of operational and archival data
We developed:
Complete case workflow engine
Biometric enrollment and verification system
Android-based field verification application
Hearing and notice management system
Secure document and evidence repository
Synchronization and VPN-based connectivity framework
Role-based access management
Supervisory dashboards and MIS reporting
Searchable archival and document indexing platform
The operational workflow enabled police units to initiate new suspect cases through Android devices with biometric capture, including fingerprint and retina scan acquisition, geo-tagged verification, image capture, and supporting document upload. Verification data synchronized securely through VPN or police station networks to the centralized platform.
Once uploaded, tribunal workflows were automatically initiated for hearing scheduling, verification processing, order management, and case archival. Historical legacy records were scanned, indexed, metadata-tagged, and migrated into a searchable centralized repository to ensure continuity between legacy and newly created digital cases.
One of the project’s biggest engineering challenges was the migration and digitization of nearly three decades of sensitive historical records while simultaneously deploying a secure biometric-enabled workflow system across geographically distributed operational units.

Case Study: Large-Scale Legacy Data Digitization & Citizen Search Platform
1. Client Goal
A large-scale citizen verification initiative required the digitization, indexing, and statewide publication of historical archival records dating from 1951 to 1971. Earlier attempts at manual verification had faced severe operational and public-access challenges because applicants were required to physically visit district offices to search decades-old paper records. The client’s objective was to create a secure, searchable, scalable, and citizen-accessible digital platform that could make legacy records available across the state through both online and assisted access channels.
The project involved highly sensitive archival documents spread across 25 district headquarters. Many of the records, including documents over 65 years old, were in fragile and deteriorated condition. The solution needed to support millions of searches within strict timelines while ensuring high availability, multilingual accessibility, and operational reliability across geographically distributed locations.

2. What We Did
Bohniman Systems Pvt. Ltd. acted as the prime contractor for the complete DLDD (Digitization and Legacy Data Distribution) initiative, including scanning operations, software development, indexing, deployment, and statewide rollout.
The project covered:
Digitization of approximately 6.25 lakh archival pages
Processing of over 2.02 crore indexed legacy records
Management of nearly 300 GB of searchable archival data
Our field teams physically visited 25 district headquarters across the state to scan and digitize archival records, including historical registers and electoral rolls from 1951–1971. Due to the fragile nature of many records, specialized scanning workflows and handling protocols were developed to preserve document readability and integrity.
The engineering scope included:
Development of a multilingual citizen search platform
Record-level indexing and metadata tagging
High-speed search engine with phonetic and multilingual search capability
Search by name, father’s name, village, district, and legacy code
Preprocessed multilingual data storage in Assamese, Bengali, and English
Image rendering and linked archival record retrieval
Custom transliteration utilities for multilingual data preparation
Compression and optimization of archival image storage
Administrative monitoring dashboards
Local replication and deployment tools
Disaster recovery and backup mechanisms
One of the most critical engineering challenges was record-level indexing and mapping of archival images to individual records. This activity was executed using a combination of custom-built tools and a large operational workforce involving more than 200 trained personnel.
To overcome internet limitations in rural and distributed environments, Bohniman designed a hybrid deployment architecture:
Cloud-hosted citizen access portal
Distributed deployment on 5,000 laptops
Operations across 2,500 assisted service centers statewide
The same searchable database and archival image repository were made available through both online and local assisted-access environments, ensuring uninterrupted citizen access regardless of connectivity limitations.
The project was executed under strict timelines with:
15 software developers
40 scanning operators
40 field engineers
200+ support and indexing personnel
The complete statewide implementation was delivered within approximately six months, including scanning, indexing, software deployment, training, and operational rollout.

Case Study: Large-Scale Family Linkage Verification & Distributed Verification Workflow Platform
Client Goal
A large-scale citizen verification initiative required a technology platform to validate lineage relationships between applicants and historical legacy records. The objective was to identify false or inconsistent claims where legacy reference codes belonging to unrelated families were used by applicants, while also assisting statutory verification officers in conducting structured verification and hearing processes at statewide scale.
The challenge involved validating relationship linkages across millions of citizens and households within strict timelines. Since the same legacy code could legitimately appear across siblings, cousins, and multiple households, the system needed to intelligently generate relationship-based family trees and assist officers in identifying inconsistencies through structured verification workflows.
What We Did
Bohniman Systems Pvt. Ltd. acted as the prime contractor for design, development, deployment, and operational support of the distributed Family Tree Verification platform.
The system processed:
57 lakh family trees
90 lakh individual verification cases
Over 43 lakh legacy codes
Coverage across all districts of the state
Approximately 250 GB of distributed verification data
We developed:
Family tree generation engine
Relationship linkage algorithms
Verification workflow system
Hearing management modules
Case assignment engine
Reporting and dashboard systems
Distributed synchronization engine
Operator utilities and reporting tools
Offline-first district deployment architecture
The core workflow automatically generated computerized family trees whenever multiple applicants used the same legacy reference code. These family trees consolidated related applicants across different households and generations into a unified relationship structure.
Verification officers compared the system-generated family trees with manually submitted family trees collected from applicants. The platform enabled officers to identify mismatches, inconsistencies, and potential false linkage claims, after which hearings and verification workflows were initiated.
One of the biggest engineering challenges was managing large-scale relational complexity where the same historical legacy code could appear across extended family structures spanning multiple households and districts. Additional complexity arose from spelling variations and strict implementation timelines.
The solution was deployed across:
5,000 laptops
2,500 distributed service centers
27 districts simultaneously
An offline-first architecture was implemented to overcome connectivity limitations. Verification data from distributed laptop deployments were synchronized to centralized servers through scheduled replication processes.
The project was completed within an aggressive timeline:
2 months implementation
15 days statewide rollout
15 days training and operational onboarding
The deployment involved:
15 developers
10 field engineers
6 trainers
6 support personnel
6 district coordinators
Project Success
The platform successfully enabled large-scale relationship verification and assisted statutory officers in managing one of the largest distributed citizen verification workflows undertaken at the time.
Key outcomes included:
Successful generation and verification of 57 lakh computerized family trees
Processing of 90 lakh verification cases
Statewide distributed deployment across all operational districts
Efficient identification of relationship inconsistencies and false linkage claims
Structured support for hearings and verification workflows
Reliable synchronization from distributed offline environments
Stable execution under strict operational timelines and legally sensitive conditions
The project demonstrated Bohniman Systems’ capability in designing and executing complex relational-data platforms, distributed workflow systems, and large-scale verification architectures involving millions of linked records and geographically distributed operational teams.
